Abstract

[Placeholder] We present VIGA, a novel framework that treats vision as inverse graphics and employs Vision-Language Models (VLMs) as coding agents to understand, reason about, and manipulate 3D scenes. Our approach bridges the gap between 2D visual perception and 3D scene understanding by learning to generate executable code that reconstructs and modifies 3D environments. VIGA demonstrates strong performance on both static scene reconstruction and dynamic scene manipulation tasks, outperforming existing methods while providing interpretable and editable outputs. Our framework opens new possibilities for AI-assisted 3D content creation, scene editing, and embodied AI applications.
